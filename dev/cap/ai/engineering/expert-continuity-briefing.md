# Expert Continuity Briefing (MUST)

## Purpose

Transform a running discussion into a continuity brief that enables another expert or agent to continue the work without material loss of goal, reasoning state, decisions, evidence, or next actions.

## Trigger

Use when an ongoing discussion, advisory thread, or reasoning session must be transferred so another expert can continue without reconstructing the material context from scratch.

## Rules (MUST)

- Make the target goal, current problem framing, and current state of understanding explicit.
- Separate confirmed facts and decisions from assumptions, inferences, and unresolved questions.
- Preserve decision-critical evidence and source anchors when they are available.
- Capture unresolved blockers, tensions, and missing evidence explicitly instead of smoothing them away.
- Define the immediate next step so the receiving expert can begin work directly.
- State the expected role or expertise posture of the receiving expert when that materially affects continuity.
- Keep the brief selective and continuity-oriented; do not dump raw conversational residue as a substitute for structure.
- Do not collapse the discussion into a generic summary that loses working intent, rationale, or actionability.

## Fallback

- If relevant discussion segments, source anchors, or decisions are missing or contradictory, keep the brief provisional and list the continuity gaps that the receiving expert must re-check first.

## Minimal Output Contract

- target goal
- current discussion state
- confirmed decisions
- explicit assumptions and inferences
- evidence or source anchors (if available)
- unresolved questions or blockers
- immediate next step
- expected receiver role or expertise posture
- current result status (`preliminary|final`)
