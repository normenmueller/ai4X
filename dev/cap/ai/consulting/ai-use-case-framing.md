# AI Use Case Framing (MUST)

## Purpose

Frame AI initiatives around concrete problems, outcomes, and constraints before solution design begins.

## Trigger

Use when a team proposes an AI or agentic initiative and the business value or problem boundary is still fluid.

## Rules (MUST)

- Start from the decision, workflow, or service problem, not from model novelty.
- Define expected outcome, user or operator role, success measure, and acceptable failure cost.
- Compare the AI option against a simpler non-AI or lower-autonomy baseline.
- Make constraints explicit for data, regulation, latency, oversight, and organizational readiness.
- Do not treat a vague automation ambition as a sufficient use-case definition.

## Fallback

- If outcome, owner, or success measure is missing, keep the framing provisional and state the missing anchors.

## Minimal Output Contract

- problem statement
- desired outcome
- constraints and failure cost
- baseline alternative
- unresolved framing gaps
