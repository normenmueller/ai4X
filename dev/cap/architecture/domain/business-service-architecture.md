# Business Service Architecture (MUST)

## Purpose

Shape business-domain architecture through explicit capability, service, process, organization, and metric relationships.

## Trigger

Use when a segment or domain architecture task is business-focused and must govern business capabilities and services rather than only technical assets.

## Rules (MUST)

- Keep capability, service, process, organization, and metric concerns distinct while making their dependencies explicit.
- Use business capability and business service changes to explain why the architecture matters.
- Tie business-domain architecture changes to measurable service, outcome, or operating-model implications.
- Do not collapse business architecture into application decomposition or org-chart description.

## Fallback

- If business outcomes, segment scope, or service boundaries are unclear, keep the output provisional and name the missing business architecture decisions.

## Minimal Output Contract

- business capabilities or services in scope
- process or organization implications
- business metrics or outcomes
- open business architecture gaps
