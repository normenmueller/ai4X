# Goal Quality Discipline (MUST)

## Purpose

Shape goals so they are material, outcome-oriented, understandable, owned, and usable for steering rather than vague aspiration or activity tracking.

## Trigger

Use when goals, objectives, outcomes, or target statements are proposed, reviewed, prioritized, or challenged.

## Rules (MUST)

- A goal must name the desired outcome, not merely the work to be performed.
- A goal must be material enough to justify attention, ownership, review, and trade-offs.
- A goal must be understandable to its intended audience without private context.
- A goal must have an accountable owner or owning team.
- A goal must fit the relevant strategic, product, operational, or organizational context.
- A goal must be bounded enough that progress and completion can be judged.
- Do not accept goals that combine multiple unrelated outcomes into one statement.
- Do not accept goals that are only slogans, tasks, deliverables, or wishes.
- If a goal depends on hidden assumptions, constraints, or trade-offs, make them explicit before using the goal for steering.

## Fallback

- If the outcome, owner, scope, or decision relevance is unclear, keep the goal provisional and name the missing goal-quality basis.

## Minimal Output Contract

- goal statement
- desired outcome
- rationale or priority logic
- owner or owning team
- scope or boundary
- evidence needed to judge progress or completion
