# Goal Cascade and Alignment (MUST)

## Purpose

Align goals across levels, teams, or domains so local goals contribute to shared intent without mechanical cascading, hidden conflicts, or ownership dilution.

## Trigger

Use when goals are translated, cascaded, aligned, decomposed, or reviewed across teams, levels, portfolios, or organizational boundaries.

## Rules (MUST)

- Preserve the intent of the higher-level goal when deriving lower-level goals.
- Make the contribution logic explicit; do not assume alignment from hierarchy or naming similarity.
- A lower-level goal must have local ownership and controllable contribution, not merely inherited wording.
- Do not cascade goals mechanically when teams need different contribution paths.
- Do not create direct dependency chains that make accountability ambiguous.
- Identify conflicts, trade-offs, duplicate goals, and unsupported goals before accepting the aligned set.
- Keep shared goals explicit when multiple teams contribute to the same outcome.
- Distinguish alignment from subordination: a goal may support, constrain, or guardrail another goal without being a child goal.

## Fallback

- If contribution logic, ownership, or conflict handling is unclear, keep the alignment provisional and name the missing cascade basis.

## Minimal Output Contract

- source goal
- aligned or derived goal
- contribution logic
- owner or owning team for each goal
- dependencies or guardrails
- conflicts or trade-offs
