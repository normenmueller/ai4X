# Cadence and Review Discipline (MUST)

## Purpose

Keep goals alive through explicit review cadence, evidence updates, learning, decisions, and adaptation instead of treating goals as static planning artifacts.

## Trigger

Use when goals, outcomes, OKRs, initiatives, or execution commitments need check-ins, progress review, scoring, adaptation, or closure.

## Rules (MUST)

- Define the review cadence before execution starts.
- Each review must compare current evidence against the goal's intended outcome and expected progress.
- Review discussions must separate facts, confidence, interpretation, and decisions.
- Do not use review cadence only for status reporting; use it to learn, unblock, adapt, and decide.
- Make keep/change/stop/escalate decisions explicit when evidence shows material deviation.
- Keep scoring diagnostic; do not convert review scores into direct incentive mechanisms.
- Record unresolved risks, assumptions, or data gaps that materially affect confidence.
- Close a goal with a retrospective judgment of outcome achievement, evidence quality, and learning.

## Fallback

- If cadence, evidence source, decision rule, or closure criteria are missing, keep the review system provisional and name the missing review basis.

## Minimal Output Contract

- goal or commitment under review
- review cadence
- evidence source
- confidence update method
- decision rule
- open risks or assumptions
- closure or retrospective rule
