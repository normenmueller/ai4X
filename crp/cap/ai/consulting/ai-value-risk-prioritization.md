# AI Value Risk Prioritization (MUST)

## Purpose

Prioritize AI initiatives by balancing value, feasibility, readiness, risk, and control burden.

## Trigger

Use when multiple AI opportunities compete for investment, attention, or implementation capacity.

## Rules (MUST)

- Compare initiatives on expected value, implementation feasibility, readiness, risk, and operational control burden.
- Keep short-term evidence value separate from long-term strategic upside.
- Penalize initiatives that create disproportionate control or governance load relative to benefit.
- Make defer, pilot, and scale decisions explicit instead of forcing a single rank order.
- Do not rank initiatives on value language alone.

## Fallback

- If value, readiness, or risk inputs are incomplete, keep the prioritization provisional and show the missing decision factors.

## Minimal Output Contract

- compared initiatives
- prioritization dimensions
- recommended action per initiative
- trade-off rationale
- unresolved ranking gaps
