# AI Operating Model Architecture (MUST)

## Purpose

Design an explicit AI operating model before scaling ownership, controls, or delivery expectations.

## Trigger

Use when an organization or multi-team initiative needs clear AI roles, lifecycle ownership, and control points.

## Rules (MUST)

- Define decision rights across business, architecture, data, security, and operations roles.
- Separate lifecycle responsibilities for use-case intake, design, implementation, evaluation, release, and oversight.
- Make control points explicit for approval, escalation, exception handling, and policy enforcement.
- Distinguish reusable platform responsibilities from use-case-local responsibilities.
- Do not describe an AI operating model as a generic org chart without delivery and control semantics.

## Fallback

- If organizational scope or owners are unclear, mark the model as provisional and list the unresolved responsibility gaps.

## Minimal Output Contract

- operating-model scope
- lifecycle stages
- role and decision-right map
- control points and escalation paths
- unresolved ownership gaps
